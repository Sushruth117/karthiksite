A Poem by Bharathiyar

In one of our podcasts, Karthik read aloud and discussed a profound poem by the great Tamil poet Bharathiyar. His interpretation and passion for this piece showed his deep connection to literature and philosophy.

தேடிச் சோறுநிதந் தின்று

பல சின்னஞ் சிறுகதைகள் பேசி

மனம் வாடித் துன்பமிக உழன்று

பிறர் வாட பலசெயல்கள் செய்து

நரை கூடிக் கிழப்பருவ மெய்தி

கொடுங் கூட்டுக் கிரையெனப்பின் மாயும்

பல வேடிக்கை மனிதரைப் போலே

நான் வீழ்வேனோ எனு நினைத்தாயோ?

Translation

"Scavenging for food daily,
Engaging in many petty, insignificant talks,
With minds wilted by sorrow and suffering,
Performing actions that bring misery to others,
Growing old with graying hair but achieving nothing,
Eventually succumbing to the cruel fate of death,
Just like these laughable humans,
Did you think I too would fall?"

This poem encapsulates the rebellious spirit that Karthik embodied—refusing to settle for mediocrity and always striving for something greater.

A Love for Physics

Karthik was one of the rare individuals who would pick up the Feynman Lectures on Physics for fun reading. While most people read novels or magazines during their leisure time, Karthik found joy in understanding the fundamental principles of the universe.

His eyes would light up when discussing quantum mechanics, relativity, or even the simple beauty of classical mechanics. He had the remarkable ability to explain complex physics concepts in ways that made them accessible and exciting to everyone around him.

"Physics isn't just equations—it's the language the universe speaks. Once you understand it, you see beauty everywhere." — Karthik, during a late-night discussion
3

Passion for Innovation & Entrepreneurship

Karthik firmly believed in the power of ideas and entrepreneurship to change the world. His presentations were always insightful, thought-provoking, and grounded in deep research.

In this presentation from his IEP project, Karthik shared insightful thoughts on the crucial role of innovation and entrepreneurship in the Indian economy. His analysis highlighted how essential these elements are for driving economic growth and job creation, particularly in the face of shifting global trends.

He effectively articulated the current landscape of entrepreneurship in India, noting both the progress made in urban centers like Bangalore and the challenges faced by sectors such as agriculture and rural economies. His recommendations for improving business incubators and strengthening intellectual property rights were not only timely but also reflected a deep understanding of the barriers to innovation in our country.

4

A Friend to All

Beyond his intellectual pursuits, Karthik was a genuine friend who always made time for those he cared about. Whether it was a late-night philosophical discussion, helping someone understand a difficult concept, or simply sharing a laugh over coffee, he brought warmth and joy to every interaction.

His kindness, humility, and infectious enthusiasm for life touched everyone who knew him. He had the rare gift of making each person feel heard, valued, and inspired.
